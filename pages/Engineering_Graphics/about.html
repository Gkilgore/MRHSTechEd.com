About

Hi! I'm Mrs. Russo. I teach Engineering Graphics at MHS, where I help students explore the fundamentals of 2D and 3D design. The course focuses on both virtual and physical modeling, with hands-on projects like bridge building and 3D printing.


Onshape Preview

Onshape is a cloud-based design software that allows you to create 3D models and drawings from scratch. It’s like using digital tools to build objects, but all your work is saved online, so you can access it from anywhere. It’s used by engineers and designers to create everything from simple objects to complex machines. Since it’s cloud-based, you can collaborate with others and access your projects from any device, making teamwork easy. Curriculum Outline



Unit Title Unit Goals Recommended Duration
Unit 1: Introduction to Design & Self-Management
  • CG5: Safe and informed decisions when selecting and using equipment/tools.
  • LG1: Apply design process concepts to develop engineering solutions.
 2 weeks
Unit 2: 2D Drawing
  • LG1: Use mechanical drawing tools and techniques for orthographic drawings.
  • LG2: Apply measurement and scale concepts for accurate visual representations.
 4-5 weeks
Unit 3: 3D Drawing
  • Apply isometric, perspective, and texture rendering concepts for accurate visual representations.
 3-4 weeks
Unit 4: Ergonomics & Materials
  • Create and defend ergonomic designs using appropriate materials and human factor guidelines.
 3-4 weeks
Unit 5: Computer-Aided Design
  • LG1: Construct unique 3D representations using CAD software.
  • LG2: Assess client needs, design solutions, and present designs.
 11-12 weeks
Unit 6: Prototyping & Presentations
  • LG1: Create 3D prototypes using CNC machines, 3D printers, or laser engravers.
  • LG2: Self-critique and critique the work of others effectively.
 3-4 weeks
Unit 7: Product Design
  • LG1: Design a product through multiple iterations of a product concept.
  • LG2: Apply drawing and design concepts to create solutions within an area of opportunity.
 5-6 weeks
