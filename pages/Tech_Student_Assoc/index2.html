ABOUT THE CLUB

The Technology Student Association Club is the oldest student membership organization dedicated exclusively to students enrolled in technology and engineering education classes in middle and high schools. It enhances personal development, leadership, and career opportunities in STEM, whereby members apply and integrate these concepts through intracurricular activities, competitions, and related programs. TSA is accelerating student achievement and supporting teachers by providing engaging opportunities to develop STEM skills.

PREVIOUS COMPETITIONS

2019 Competition

In 2019, the TSA National Robotics Competition challenged students to design and program autonomous robots capable of navigating a complex obstacle course. Held in Orlando, Florida, the event featured over 150 teams competing in precision tasks such as object retrieval and line-following navigation. The winning team from Texas impressed the judges with a robot that used AI-based pathfinding to adapt to unexpected obstacles in real time.

2024 Competition

In 2024, the competition took a futuristic turn with a Mars Exploration Robotics Challenge, where students built rovers to complete simulated planetary tasks. Hosted in Atlanta, Georgia, the event required teams to program robots for terrain mapping, rock sample collection, and autonomous decision-making.
